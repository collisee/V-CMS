A charming, quiet Hanoi in Bui Xuan Phai’s paintings

VietNamNet Bridge – Steven Tait, 35, from Canada impressed the jury of the contest about Thang Long-Hanoi’s 1000 History by an entry written on Vietnamese non la (palm-leaf conical hats).

The entry of Cavico company.

Tait has been working for a UN project in Vietnam for over a year. He speaks Vietnamese very well. He said he loves Vietnam and Vietnamese people for their hospitality. In his eyes, Vietnamese girls are very beautiful and charming in ao dai and non la.

He decided to write an essay in Vietnamese about Hanoi on five non la to submit to the contest about Thang Long-Hanoi’s 1000 years, which was held from October 11, 2009 to August 31, 2010.

Tait is the only foreign contestant.

Steven Tait and his entry on non la.

The organizing board said besides Tait’s entry, there were many special entries, including a 28m long entry of the youth society of Cavico Vietnam company and two entries written as calligraphic works of the Hanoi Children’s Palace, etc.

The awards ceremony will be held on October 1 in Hanoi.
